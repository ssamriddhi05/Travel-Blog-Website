MAHARASHTRA

Maharashtra is one of India's largest commercial and industrial centres, which has led to its being called the gateway of India. Maharashtra is also famous for its culture and beauty. The ancient cave paintings found at Ajanta and Ellora are UNESCO World Heritage sites and popular tourist destinations. Music, dance, Bollywood, festivals, and much more contribute to this lively culture.It attracts tourists from other Indian states and foreign countries.[1] It was the second most visited Indian state by foreigners and fifth most visited state by domestic tourists in the country in 2021.[2] Aurangabad is the tourism capital of Maharashtra.[3] Winter, from October to February, is the best time to visit Maharashtra when the weather is cool and pleasant. Temperatures range from 12-13°C in Lonavala to 22-30°C in Mumbai, offering ideal conditions for outdoor activities and weekend trips.


GUJARAT

Gujarat is one of the most diverse States in India. Its history stretches over a long years from the age old Harappan Civilization to the Mughal period.Tourism in Gujarat offers a Traveler's Paradise. It offers a wide spectrum of Tourism sectors which sing in the glory of Gujarat's rich culture and heritage. The state is famous for its majestic temples like the regal Sun Temple in Modhera, the Somnath Temple, and the Dwarkadhish Temple. These temples are not only religious sites but also architectural wonders that will leave you in awe of their intricate carvings and stunning craftsmanship. October to February is the best season to explore the best tourist places in Gujarat. With pleasant weather, the places to visit in Gujaratbecome easy for sightseeing. July to September is also a great time to explore Gujarat famous places. Some of the cities get less to no rainfall at all.


JAMMU & KASHMIR

Assam's most famous attraction is undoubtedly Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. In Assam, places like Jorhat, Tinsukia, and Dibrugarh will let you experience life in the tea gardens. Assam is home to several ancient temples, which are not only architectural marvels but also important spiritual centres. The state of Assam is best visited from November to May. The weather is perfect, with cool winds blowing from the Himalayan foothills to reduce heat and humidity.
